Watching K-Speech

A Visual Korean Phoneme Simulation with Extended Vocal Tract Lab

Extended VTL specifically enhances the portrayal of sounds prevalent in Korean speech by using the newly added “w” and “V” rather than using “u” and “o” that were not adequate to represent Korean sound.

Abstract




This study introduces a novel methodology for visualizing the vocal tract of Korean dysarthria patients through a customized version of the Vocal Tract Lab (VTL). By transforming audio files into the SAMPA phoneme format, this approach leverages our extended VTL to generate visual representations of speech. The enhancements to the original VTL include the addition of five new vowels, seven diphthongs, nine consonants, and six modified phonemes. Evaluations conducted with linguistics experts validated the utility of our visualization technique in aiding the treatment of speech disorders, though they also highlighted limitations, which affects the precise representation of certain Korean consonants. Despite these challenges, the extended VTL model shows promise for application in the study of languages typically not much addressed in the language processing field. This work offers a tool for clinicians and researchers to enhance diagnostic and therapeutic strategies for speech disorders.
